Students will be able to opt in to the Eagle Express plan at that time.</p><h4><a id="room-board-rates">&nbsp;</a>Are room and board rates included in the plan?</h4><p>Room and board rates are charged separately and in addition to academic costs.</p><h4><a id="course-instructional-fees">&nbsp;</a>Are course/instructional fees included in the cost?</h4><p>The Eagle Express locked-in cost includes course/instructional fees. In the traditional plan, course/instructional fees are not included in the fixed cost and they will vary.</p><h4><a id="fin-aid-scholarships">&nbsp;</a>Will the Eagle Express plan affect my eligibility for or my amount of financial aid and/or scholarships?</h4><p>Questions about <a href="https://financialaid.unt.edu/">financial aid or scholarships</a> should be addressed by calling 844-236-6855.</p><h4><a id="graduate-4-years">&nbsp;</a>What happens if I donâ€™t graduate in my allotted time under the Eagle Express plan?</h4><p>If you donâ€™t meet the conditions for the graduation incentive for the Eagle Express plan, you end up paying about the same as you would under the traditional tuition plan. If you start in Fall 2017, for example, you would pay $52 less under the Eagle Express plan over four years without the incentives than you would on the traditional tuition plan. So it really does pay to graduate on time, but you wonâ€™t be penalized if you donâ€™t. If you don't graduate within the allotted time, you will move to the traditional plan effective for the next semester.</p><h4><a id="automatic-incentive">&nbsp;</a>Will I automatically receive the graduation incentive Iâ€™ve earned or do I have to request it?</h4><p>By applying for graduation prior to your final 15 hours before graduation from UNT, the amount of your Eagle Express incentive can be verified and credited to your student account. <!-- Please go to How to apply for the Eagle Express Incentive for more information. --></p><h4><a id="when-receive-rebate">&nbsp;</a>How and when will I receive the statewide $1,000 tuition rebate?</h4><p>You must apply for the <a href="https://registrar.unt.edu/graduation-and-diplomas/1000-tuition-rebate">statewide tuition rebate</a>. The rebate may take up to 60 days after graduation to process.</p><h4><a id="how-four-years">&nbsp;</a>How can I graduate in four years?</h4><p>To graduate in four years, youâ€™ll need to earn 30 college credit hours each year. UNTâ€™s tuition schedule lets you take 15 hours per semester for about the same cost as 12 hours. If you choose the new Eagle Express Tuition Plan your first semester, you can also save up to $3,000 your final 15 hours if you graduate on time.</p><h4><a id="summer-classes">&nbsp;</a>To graduate in four years, UNT needs to offer a wide variety of required courses every semester, including summer. Do you have plans to make all required classes available for each major every semester?</h4><p>UNT is making every effort to offer the necessary courses throughout the academic year and has focused specifically on improving and enhancing its summer offerings.</p><h4><a id="traditional-plan">&nbsp;</a>If I donâ€™t choose the Eagle Express plan, what is my tuition and fee rate?</h4><p>If you donâ€™t opt into the Eagle Express plan, you will automatically be part of the traditional plan, in which your academic costs will increase 3.9 percent each year for four years and course/instructional fees will vary.</p><h4><br /><a id="dual-ap-credit">&nbsp;</a>How does the Eagle Express plan affect my dual credit, AP, IB or CLEP hours?</h4><p>The Eagle Express graduation incentive is based on how many hours you complete at UNT. Any college credit hours you earned before high school graduation do not count against new freshmen. For example, if youâ€™re a high school graduate with 40 AP and/or dual credit hours, you would still be eligible for the $3,000 graduation incentive â€“ which is a fabulous deal.</p><h4><a id="transfer-students">&nbsp;</a>Can I transfer in hours and still qualify for the Eagle Express plan? Is there a limit to the number of transfer hours?</h4><p>Yes and yes. The Eagle Express plan is based on you completing most of your coursework at UNT, but it does allow you to transfer in a certain amount of hours. But, you will only qualify for the graduation incentive if you meet the terms noted on the <a href="/transfer">transfer page</a>.</p><h4><a id="can-i-leave-semester">&nbsp;</a>Can I enroll under the Eagle Express plan, leave school for a semester or year, and return to the plan?</h4><p>Yes, provided you graduate within four years of the time you opt in to the Eagle Express plan.</p><h4><a id="change-my-mind">&nbsp;</a>What if I change my mind after I choose a tuition plan?</h4><p>Eligible students can choose a tuition plan or alter their decision until the payment deadline for the semester. Military hours are not required to be considered for admission to UNT. If you supply military credit, the admissions office must receive a memo from your academic advisor specifying what work you want applied toward your degree. Military credits posted to your transcript are considered <a href="/transfer">transfer hours</a>.&nbsp;For more information, contact <a href="http://www.unt.edu/veterans">Student Veterans Services</a>.</p><h4><a id="total-credit-hours">&nbsp;</a>Will my total number of credit hours be considered for the incentive or just the hours that apply toward my degree?</h4><p>Your total hours apply to the Eagle Express plan.</p><h4><a id="complete-degree">&nbsp;</a>If I sign up for the Eagle Express Tuition Plan but don't complete my degree at UNT, will I be billed for the difference between the plans?</h4><p>No, you won't be charged.</p><h4><a id="summer-cost">&nbsp;</a>Why would taking courses in the summer result in a higher total cost?</h4><p>The cost comparison graphs on the first page of this site are based on taking 15 semester hours in the fall semester and 15 semester hours in the spring semester. This takes full advantage of UNTâ€™s tuition schedule, which lets you take 15 hours in a semester for about the same cost as 12 hours. In the shorter summer sessions, fewer hours are taken so there is no price break. Mandatory fees are charged for each summer session. Taking a few summer courses to graduate a semester early is a smart move, but Eagle Express students who regularly take 12 hours in the fall, 12 hours in the spring and 6 in the summer may find that the higher total cost offsets their graduation incentive.</p><p>The most cost-effective course of action is to choose Eagle Express, take 15 hours each semester and graduate on
